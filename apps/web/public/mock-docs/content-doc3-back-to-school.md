# Back to School 2026 Campaign Brief -- "Ready for the New Term"

**Document Type:** Promotional / Seasonal Campaign
**Author:** Marketing Director
**Campaign Code:** JD-BTS-26001
**Campaign Period:** 1 August 2026 to 12 September 2026
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL -- INTERNAL USE ONLY

---

## 1. Campaign Overview

The Back to School campaign is the second most important trading period of the year after Christmas, generating approximately GBP 480 million in revenue across the six-week campaign window. This represents approximately 9% of full-year revenue concentrated into 11% of the year.

The campaign objective is to achieve revenue of GBP 480 million with a footfall uplift of 8% versus the prior year. The target average order value is GBP 78.00, reflecting a mix of school footwear purchases and parent top-up baskets.

The target customer is parents of children aged 4 to 16, with a secondary audience of university students aged 18 to 21 purchasing their own wardrobe for the new term.

---

## 2. Hero Products

Ten hero products are designated for the campaign, with dedicated marketing support, enhanced stock allocation, and prominent in-store positioning.

Nike Air Force 1 Low (GBP 100 RRP). Target volume of 45,000 pairs. This is the single most important SKU for the campaign. The white colourway is a school uniform staple across the UK and consistently sells through within the first three weeks of August.

Adidas Gazelle (GBP 85 RRP). Target volume of 38,000 pairs. The Gazelle has seen a resurgence in younger demographics driven by terrace fashion trends.

Nike Dunk Low (GBP 105 RRP). Target volume of 32,000 pairs. Popular with secondary school age children as a fashion statement beyond uniform requirements.

Converse Chuck Taylor All Star (GBP 60 RRP). Target volume of 28,000 pairs. The entry price point for iconic footwear, popular with both school and university age groups.

Vans Old Skool (GBP 65 RRP). Target volume of 22,000 pairs. Consistent performer across all age groups.

Nike Tech Fleece Full Zip Hoodie (GBP 120 RRP). Target volume of 18,000 units. The premium casual layer for older students.

Adidas Originals 3-Stripes Tracksuit (GBP 95 RRP). Target volume of 15,000 units. A perennial favourite for the back to school wardrobe.

The North Face Nuptse Jacket (GBP 265 RRP). Target volume of 8,000 units. Early season outerwear for the autumn term, particularly important in Scotland and the North of England.

Under Armour Backpack (GBP 45 RRP). Target volume of 25,000 units. An essential accessory for school and university.

Nike Elite Socks 3-Pack (GBP 18 RRP). Target volume of 40,000 packs. A high-volume, low-value add-on purchase with strong margin.

## 3. Promotional Mechanics

Three core promotional mechanics will operate during the campaign period.

20% off full-price footwear runs from 15 August to 31 August. This mechanic applies to all full-price footwear, including the hero SKUs. The discount is funded jointly by JD and the relevant brands under existing markdown support agreements. The expected margin impact on promoted footwear is a reduction of 5 to 6 percentage points versus full-price margin.

3 for 2 on accessories runs for the full campaign period from 1 August to 12 September. This mechanic applies to socks, caps, bags, and shoe care products. Accessories have high margins (51% to 55%), so a 3 for 2 promotion still delivers positive gross margin. The mechanic also drives units per transaction, which supports average order value.

10% student discount via Student Beans runs for the full campaign period. This is a permanent discount mechanic for verified students and is available year-round, but is heavily promoted during Back to University in September.

## 4. Media and Marketing Spend

The total media budget for the Back to School campaign is GBP 3.8 million, allocated across six channels.

Google Ads accounts for GBP 1.2 million, or 32% of the budget. The target return on ad spend is 4.2 times. Campaigns target key search terms including "school trainers," "back to school shoes," and "kids Nike trainers."

Meta, including Facebook and Instagram, accounts for GBP 1.0 million, or 26% of the budget. The target return on ad spend is 5.0 times. Creative content focuses on parent decision-makers with messaging around durability, value, and style.

TikTok accounts for GBP 800,000, or 21% of the budget. The target return on ad spend is 6.5 times. Content is targeted at teenagers and young adults with influencer partnerships and trend-driven creative.

Television advertising accounts for GBP 500,000, or 13% of the budget. A 30-second spot will air across ITV, Channel 4, and Sky during family viewing slots in the two weeks before schools return.

Influencer partnerships account for GBP 300,000, or 8% of the budget. Ten family and lifestyle influencers with a combined reach of 3 million followers will create content featuring the hero products.

## 5. Stock Requirements

Total stock allocation for the campaign is 1.8 million units across all categories and brands.

Hero SKU stock is allocated to all 472 stores plus the online fulfilment centre. Allocation follows a tiered model where flagship stores receive 100% of the hero SKU range, A tier stores receive 80%, B tier stores 60%, and C tier stores 40%.

A buffer of 15% above forecast demand is held at the Kingwood and Rochdale distribution centres for hero SKUs only. This buffer is designed to cover unexpected demand spikes and to enable rapid replenishment of fast-selling sizes and colourways.

Supplier lead times are critical for campaign success. Nike has a 14-day lead time for core franchise product, Adidas has a 12-day lead time, and Vans and Converse each have a 10-day lead time. All campaign stock must be delivered to the DCs by 25 July to allow for allocation and distribution to stores before the 1 August launch.

---

## 6. In-Store Execution

All 472 stores will have dedicated Back to School window schemes and in-store point of sale materials. The largest 200 stores by revenue will also have dedicated Back to School zones with enhanced fixtures and digital screens.

Colleague training materials are distributed to all stores by 20 July. Training covers the promotional mechanics, hero product knowledge, and upselling techniques, particularly around the 3 for 2 accessories promotion.

Early access for JD Status members runs from 8 August to 14 August, one week before the general promotion starts. This provides a benefit to loyalty programme members and helps to smooth demand across the campaign period.

---

## 7. Staffing Plan

An additional 250 temporary colleagues will be recruited for the Kingwood and Rochdale distribution centres to handle the increased volume of stock movement. These colleagues are contracted from 15 July to 30 September.

An additional 120 in-store colleagues will be deployed across the 100 highest-traffic stores for the peak Saturdays in August. These colleagues are scheduled for 8-hour shifts during the busiest trading hours of 10am to 6pm.

The total additional labour cost for the campaign is estimated at GBP 500,000.

---

## 8. Contingency Plan

A 10% buffer stock holding is maintained at the DCs for the top 20 SKUs by revenue. If any SKU sells through 80% of its allocated stock before 25 August, an automatic replenishment order is triggered from the buffer.

An overnight airfreight option is pre-approved for the top 5 SKUs, with a budget of GBP 40,000 for emergency replenishment. This option is only used if DC stock is exhausted and a stock-out is imminent in flagship stores.

A dual-carrier agreement is in place with both DPD and UPS for the campaign period. If one carrier fails to meet service levels, volume is automatically switched to the alternative carrier.

If website traffic exceeds 5 times the normal daily volume, the site auto-scales through the cloud infrastructure, with certain non-essential features (product recommendations, personalised content) temporarily disabled to prioritise checkout performance.

## 9. Success Metrics and KPIs

Campaign revenue target of GBP 480 million, equivalent to 9% of full-year revenue across 6 weeks.
Like-for-like sales growth of 5% versus the prior year Back to School campaign.
Average order value of GBP 78.00, up from GBP 75.00 in the prior year.
Footfall uplift of 8% versus the prior year across the store estate.
Conversion rate of 22% in stores and 5.2% online.
Return on ad spend of 5.0 times across all media channels blended.
JD Status member acquisition of 40,000 new paying members.
Customer satisfaction score of 4.2 out of 5 or higher.
