# Nike Framework Agreement 2026-2029 -- JD Sports UK & Ireland

**Document Type:** Supplier / Vendor Agreement
**Parties:** JD Sports Fashion plc (UK) and Nike EU Operations Limited
**Effective Date:** 1 February 2026
**Expiry Date:** 31 January 2029
**Classification:** HIGHLY RESTRICTED -- COMMERCIALLY SENSITIVE

---

## 1. Agreement Overview

This Framework Agreement governs the wholesale supply of Nike-branded product by Nike EU Operations Limited to JD Sports Fashion plc for resale in the United Kingdom and Republic of Ireland. The agreement renews and replaces the prior agreement dated 1 February 2023.

JD Sports is designated as a Tier 0 retail partner, the highest partnership tier in Nike's European distribution structure. This designation provides JD with preferential access to limited-release product, exclusive colourways, and first-market allocation on key launches.

The agreement runs for an initial term of three years, with automatic renewal for successive one-year periods unless either party provides 18 months written notice of non-renewal.

---

## 2. Territory and Exclusivity

The territory comprises the United Kingdom and the Republic of Ireland. Within this territory, JD Sports holds exclusive retail rights for Nike product in the following channels.

JD has exclusive rights to operate Nike shop-in-shops within its own stores, with a minimum of 85 shop-in-shops required across the territory by the end of year two. JD also has exclusive rights to sell Nike product in designated retail park and high street catchments, meaning no other Nike multi-brand retailer may be located within 0.5 miles of a JD store in those locations.

Nike retains the right to operate its own direct-to-consumer channels, including Nike.com, the Nike app, and Nike-owned stores (including Nike Town London and the Nike Oxford Street flagship) without restriction.

JD is granted 24 exclusive colourway SKUs per season, for a total of 96 exclusive SKUs per year. These are Nike product colour variants produced solely for JD and not available through any other retailer in the territory. Exclusivity periods range from 6 to 12 months from first delivery, after which Nike may release the colourway to other channels.

---

## 3. Trading Terms and Pricing

Nike grants JD a trade discount of 49% off the recommended retail price on core lifestyle product, including Air Force 1, Air Max, and Dunk franchises. Running product is discounted at 47% off RRP. Premium and Jordan Brand product is discounted at 44% off RRP.

Payment terms are 60 days from the end of the month of invoice. A 2% settlement discount applies if payment is made within 30 days of invoice date.

All pricing is quoted in GBP and is fixed for the duration of each seasonal order book. Nike may adjust RRP recommendations once per season, with 90 days notice.

---

## 4. Volume Commitment and Rebates

JD commits to minimum annual purchases of GBP 650 million at wholesale cost, equating to approximately GBP 1.27 billion at retail selling price. The commitment is phased quarterly as follows.

Q1 (February to April). GBP 145 million.
Q2 (May to July). GBP 160 million.
Q3 (August to October). GBP 170 million.
Q4 (November to January). GBP 175 million.

A tiered rebate structure applies based on annual purchase volume, calculated on a rolling 12-month basis and paid quarterly in arrears.

At GBP 600 million to GBP 649 million, the rebate rate is 2.0% of total purchases, paid retrospectively to the first pound. At GBP 650 million to GBP 699 million, the rate is 3.5%. At GBP 700 million and above, the rate increases to 5.0%.

As of the most recent measurement period ending October 2026, JD's rolling 12-month purchases stand at approximately GBP 645 million. This is GBP 5 million short of the 3.5% rebate threshold. Achieving the Q4 commitment of GBP 175 million is critical to reaching the 3.5% tier for the full year.

Rebate income is recognised as a reduction in cost of goods sold. The estimated annual rebate at the 3.5% tier is GBP 22.8 million.

---

## 5. Markdown Support

Nike provides markdown support to protect JD's gross margin on promoted and clearance product.

For seasonal markdowns of up to 30% off the original retail price, Nike reimburses JD for 50% of the markdown cost. For example, if a product originally retailing at GBP 100 is marked down to GBP 70, the markdown cost is GBP 30 per unit and Nike reimburses GBP 15.

For deeper markdowns between 30% and 50% off, Nike's contribution reduces to 30% of markdown cost. For markdowns exceeding 50% off, JD bears the full cost.

Nike caps total markdown support at 3% of JD's annual Nike purchases. At the planned purchase volume of GBP 650 million, this cap is GBP 19.5 million per year. If markdown claims exceed this cap, JD bears the excess in full.

Markdown claims are submitted monthly and reconciled quarterly. Nike reserves the right to audit markdown activity with 14 days notice.

---

## 6. Allocation and Ranging Rights

As a Tier 0 partner, JD receives preferential allocation on limited-release and high-demand product.

For Tier 1 launches, defined as product with expected sell-through of 85% or more within four weeks, JD receives allocation for a minimum of 200 store doors across the territory. For Tier 2 launches, the minimum door count is 150 stores. JD also receives first access to product 7 days before general release on up to 36 SKUs per year, allowing JD to capture enthusiast demand before the wider market.

JD is guaranteed supply of core franchise product, defined as Air Force 1, Air Max 1, Air Max 90, Air Max 95, Air Max 270, Dunk Low, Dunk High, and the Pegasus running franchise, at volumes sufficient to maintain 8 weeks of cover across the estate.

Nike operates a flow replenishment model for approximately 70% of JD's core product, meaning stock is automatically replenished based on sell-through data. The remaining 30% is push allocation, where JD selects quantities upfront each season.

## 7. Co-op Marketing

Nike provides a cooperative marketing fund of GBP 14 million per year to support joint marketing activities. The fund is split across four areas.

National media, including television, out-of-home advertising, and digital campaigns, accounts for GBP 6 million. Nike contributes 70% and JD contributes 30%.

In-store visual merchandising, including fixtures, signage, and digital screens for shop-in-shops, accounts for GBP 3.5 million. Nike funds this entirely.

JD digital channels, including the JD app, email marketing, and social media campaigns, account for GBP 2.5 million. Nike and JD share this equally at 50% each.

Launch events, including Air Max Day, Jordan retro drops, and athlete appearances, account for GBP 2 million. Nike contributes 60% and JD contributes 40%.

Co-op funds not utilised within the financial year lapse at year end. As of October 2026, approximately GBP 9.5 million of the FY2027 co-op budget had been committed or spent.

---

## 8. Service Level Agreements

Nike commits to an on-time, in-full delivery rate of 95% or above. Late delivery is measured against agreed delivery windows for each seasonal order book. If the on-time rate falls below 92% in any quarter, the rebate rate for that quarter is reduced by 1 percentage point.

Order lead time is 8 to 12 weeks for core product and 4 to 6 weeks for fast-track replenishment orders.

The defect allowance is 1.5% of units delivered. Product exceeding this defect rate is replaced by Nike at no cost to JD, including return shipping.

JD may return unsold product to Nike, subject to a cap of 3% of annual purchase volume. Returns beyond this cap reduce markdown support by 50% for the following season.

Invoice accuracy is targeted at 98% or above. Disputed invoices are resolved within 30 calendar days.

## 9. Product Tiers and Expected Margins

Nike product is managed across five tiers within the JD range, each with different margin characteristics.

Lifestyle product, including Air Force 1, Air Max, and Dunk, is the largest tier at 42% of Nike revenue. Gross margin is 48% to 50%. Sell-through rates are the highest in the range at 85% to 95% over a season.

Running product, including Pegasus, Vomero, and Invincible, represents 28% of Nike revenue. Gross margin is 46% to 48%. Sell-through rates are 65% to 75%.

Training product, including Metcon and SuperRep, represents 12% of Nike revenue. Gross margin is 46% to 47%. Sell-through rates are lower at 50% to 60%.

Football product, including Mercurial, Phantom, and Tiempo, represents 10% of Nike revenue. Gross margin is 45% to 47%. Seasonal sell-through depends heavily on the football calendar.

Basketball and Jordan Brand product, including Air Jordan retros, represents 8% of Nike revenue. Gross margin is 43% to 45%, the narrowest of any tier, but sell-through rates are exceptionally high at 90% to 98% driven by collector demand.

## 10. Compliance and Governance

Both parties confirm compliance with the UK Modern Slavery Act 2015. Nike provides annual audited statements on supply chain labour standards.

Both parties comply with the UK Packaging Regulations and the Plastic Packaging Tax. Nike reports quarterly on packaging reduction progress.

Nike warrants that all product supplied meets UK and EU product safety standards, including CE marking and REACH chemical regulations.

Data protection is governed by a separate Data Processing Agreement compliant with UK GDPR. Customer data shared between the parties for marketing purposes requires explicit opt-in consent.

The agreement is governed by English law. Disputes are resolved through binding arbitration in London.
