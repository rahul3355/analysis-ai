# Q3 2026 Regional Performance Review -- JD Sports UK

**Document Type:** Performance Report
**Period:** August 2026 to October 2026 (Periods 7 to 9)
**Author:** Trading Director
**Date:** 10 November 2026
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL -- INTERNAL USE ONLY

---

## 1. Executive Summary

JD Sports UK achieved total revenue of GBP 1.36 billion in Q3, representing 103.8% of the GBP 1.31 billion plan and growth of 4.2% versus the prior year quarter.

Like-for-like sales growth was 3.2%, driven by strong performance in the Back to School campaign in August and continued momentum in the running footwear category. Gross margin was 47.2%, 0.6 percentage points below the plan of 47.8%, primarily due to higher-than-planned markdown activity on apparel and a mix shift towards lower-margin footwear.

Online revenue was GBP 435 million, or 32.0% of total revenue, in line with the full-year target. Online conversion improved to 5.1%, up from 4.8% in the prior year quarter.

Operating profit for the quarter was GBP 133 million, representing a 9.8% margin, in line with the full-year target.

---

## 2. Revenue Summary

The quarterly result of GBP 1.36 billion is analysed across the three periods as follows.

Period 7 covering August achieved GBP 480 million, driven by the Back to School campaign which hit 102% of its GBP 470 million revenue target for the month. The campaign benefited from strong sell-through of Nike Air Force 1, Adidas Gazelle, and the 3 for 2 accessories promotion.

Period 8 covering September achieved GBP 440 million, in line with plan. The month performed well in footwear but saw softer apparel sales as the transition from summer to autumn ranges created a gap in outerwear availability.

Period 9 covering October achieved GBP 440 million, against a plan of GBP 430 million. Performance was boosted by early cold weather driving demand for The North Face outerwear and autumn footwear styles.

## 3. Regional Performance

Regional performance was mixed, with six regions above plan and five below plan.

The North West was the strongest performing region at 105.2% of plan. Total revenue was GBP 190 million against a plan of GBP 181 million. Like-for-like sales grew 4.8%. The Manchester Arndale flagship was the standout store at 108% of plan, driven by strong Nike and Adidas performance.

London and the South East achieved 104.1% of plan with combined revenue of GBP 408 million. The Westfield London and Westfield Stratford stores both exceeded plan by 6% or more. However, several smaller high street stores in outer London boroughs were below plan, reflecting the ongoing shift of footfall towards large shopping centres.

Scotland was the weakest region at 92.5% of plan. Total revenue was GBP 128 million against a plan of GBP 138 million. Like-for-like sales declined by 1.2%. Two factors drove the underperformance. The Dundee and Inverness stores were affected by planned refurbishment closures, reducing capacity by a combined 6 trading weeks. Separately, stock allocation for the Back to School campaign was misaligned with Scottish demand patterns, with an undersupply of larger shoe sizes (UK 10 and above) which sell disproportionately strongly in Scotland.

The Glasgow Buchanan Street flagship performed well at 103% of plan, partially offsetting the regional weakness, but the overall region requires management attention in Q4.

The Midlands achieved 102.4% of plan. The Birmingham Bullring flagship was a strong performer at 106% of plan. Retail park stores in the region also performed well, with an average of 103% of plan.

The South West achieved 101.5% of plan, driven by the Bristol Cabot Circus store and strong online penetration in the region.

Yorkshire and the North East together achieved 101.8% of plan. The Leeds Trinity flagship and Newcastle Eldon Square stores both exceeded plan by 4% or more.

Wales achieved 100.5% of plan, broadly flat. Northern Ireland achieved 99.0% of plan, slightly below.

## 4. Channel Performance

Store revenue was GBP 925 million, representing 68.0% of total quarterly revenue and growth of 2.8% versus the prior year quarter. Like-for-like store sales grew 2.1%. Footfall across the estate was up 3.5% versus the prior year, with average transaction value increasing from GBP 72.00 to GBP 74.50.

Online revenue was GBP 435 million, representing 32.0% of total quarterly revenue and growth of 7.5% versus the prior year quarter. Online conversion improved to 5.1% from 4.8%, driven by improvements to the checkout process and more targeted email marketing. Average order value was GBP 84.50, up from GBP 82.00 in the prior year quarter. Click and collect accounted for 24% of online orders, consistent with the full-year target.

---

## 5. Category Performance

Footwear revenue was GBP 748 million, or 55.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 4.8%. Gross margin was 44.5%, 0.3 percentage points below plan due to deeper discounting on clearance lines. The running footwear subcategory grew 14% year on year, driven by strong sales of ASICS Gel Kayano 31, Hoka Clifton 9, and Nike Pegasus 41.

Apparel revenue was GBP 517 million, or 38.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 3.2%. Gross margin was 49.0%, 1.0 percentage point below plan due to higher markdown activity on summer clearance lines that did not sell through before the autumn range arrived. Own-brand apparel, including McKenzie and Supply & Demand, performed well with sell-through rates of 78%.

Accessories revenue was GBP 95 million, or 7.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 2.5%. Gross margin was 51.5%, in line with plan. The 3 for 2 promotion during the Back to School campaign drove strong volume in socks and caps.

---

## 6. Brand Performance

Nike revenue was GBP 544 million, representing 40.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 3.5%. Gross margin was 46.8%, in line with plan. Lifestyle product, including Air Force 1 and Air Max, sold well with sell-through rates above 80%. Running product was more challenging, with the Pegasus 41 seeing higher-than-planned promotional activity.

Adidas revenue was GBP 354 million, representing 26.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 4.0%. Gross margin was 47.5%, 0.3 percentage points above plan. The Gazelle and Samba franchises continued to perform strongly, with sell-through rates above 85%.

New Balance revenue was GBP 82 million, representing 6.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 18.0%. Gross margin was 48.0%, 1.5 percentage points above the corporate average. The 530 and 990 series both saw strong demand.

The North Face revenue was GBP 68 million, representing 5.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 6.5%. The Nuptse jacket was a top 5 SKU across the entire business in October.

Own-brand revenue was GBP 109 million, representing 8.0% of total quarterly revenue. Growth versus the prior year was 12.0%. Gross margin was 55.0%, significantly above the blended average. The McKenzie range saw particularly strong performance in the value segment.

## 7. Top 10 Products

The top 10 products by revenue for Q3 were as follows.

Nike Air Force 1 Low was the number one product with GBP 54.0 million in revenue across approximately 540,000 pairs sold. The Back to School campaign drove strong demand in August and September.

Adidas Gazelle was number two with GBP 42.0 million across approximately 494,000 pairs. The colourway expansion into pastel shades for the summer season was well received.

Nike Dunk Low was number three with GBP 39.0 million across approximately 371,000 pairs. Continued strength across all age demographics.

The North Face Nuptse Jacket was number four with GBP 30.0 million across approximately 113,000 units. Early cold weather in October drove a sales spike.

Nike Tech Fleece Full Zip Hoodie was number five with GBP 26.0 million across approximately 217,000 units. Consistent demand throughout the quarter.

Adidas Samba was number six with GBP 24.0 million across approximately 282,000 pairs. Continued the strong trend from previous seasons.

Nike Air Max 90 was number seven with GBP 23.0 million across approximately 177,000 pairs. Core lifestyle franchise with steady demand.

Converse Chuck Taylor All Star was number eight with GBP 19.0 million across approximately 317,000 pairs. Strong Back to School performance.

Vans Old Skool was number nine with GBP 16.5 million across approximately 254,000 pairs. Solid performer across all regions.

New Balance 530 was number ten with GBP 15.0 million across approximately 176,000 pairs. The fastest growing SKU in the top 10, up 45% versus the prior year.

## 8. Operational Metrics

Average transaction value in stores was GBP 74.50 against a plan of GBP 73.00. Online average order value was GBP 84.50 against a plan of GBP 83.00.

Units per transaction in stores was 1.9, consistent with the prior year. Online units per transaction was 1.7.

The return rate for the quarter was 22.8%. Online returns were 28.5%, primarily driven by footwear fit issues. In-store returns were 4.5%, consistent with prior periods.

Stock at quarter end was GBP 1.28 billion at retail value, representing 9.2 weeks of cover. The target is 8.5 weeks. Aged stock over 90 days represented 9.5% of total stock value, above the target of 8.0%. The largest proportion of aged stock was in apparel, specifically summer seasonal product that did not clear before the Back to School campaign.

---

## 9. Key Issues and Corrective Actions

Three issues require management attention in Q4.

The Scotland underperformance is the most significant concern. The GBP 10 million revenue gap versus plan in the quarter was driven by store closures and stock allocation failures. Corrective actions include expediting the Dundee and Inverness refurbishments to complete by early December, rebalancing size curves for Scottish stores to increase UK 10 and UK 11 allocations, and deploying a dedicated regional marketing campaign in November and December.

The aged apparel stock position requires action. Approximately GBP 45 million of summer seasonal apparel remains unsold and is now aged over 90 days. A targeted markdown campaign with 40% to 50% discount is recommended for December to clear this stock, with an expected margin impact of GBP 2 million to GBP 3 million.

The Nike aged stock position, at GBP 18.5 million or 11.5% of total Nike stock, requires a coordinated markdown plan with Nike under the markdown support provisions of the framework agreement. The 50% markdown cost share arrangement should be utilised before any deeper discounting is implemented.

---

## 10. Outlook for Q4

Q4 is the most important quarter of the financial year, with a target of GBP 1.64 billion in revenue across November, December, and January.

November is driven by the Black Friday promotional period, with a target of GBP 560 million. December is the peak Christmas trading month at GBP 770 million. January contributes the balance through the clearance period.

The Scotland recovery plan, the aged stock clearance programme, and continued investment in the running footwear category are the three key priorities for the quarter.
