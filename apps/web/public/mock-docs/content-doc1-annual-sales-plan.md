# FY2027 Annual Sales Plan -- JD Sports UK

**Document Type:** Strategy / Annual Plan
**Author:** Trading Director
**Date:** February 2026
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL
**Financial Year:** FY2027 (1 February 2026 to 31 January 2027)

---

## 1. Executive Summary

JD Sports UK targets total revenue of GBP 5.3 billion in FY2027, representing growth of 3.9% versus the FY2026 outturn of GBP 5.1 billion. Like-for-like sales growth is planned at 2.5%, with the balance coming from new store openings (8 new stores) and the full-year benefit of 12 store refits completed in FY2026.

Gross margin is planned at 47.8%, broadly flat year on year. The operating margin target is 9.8%, reflecting continued operational leverage from the store estate and growing online profitability.

Online penetration is targeted at 33.0% of total UK revenue, up from 32.0% in FY2026.

---

## 2. Market Context

The UK sportswear market is estimated at GBP 8.5 billion for FY2027, growing at 3.0% to 4.0% annually. JD Sports UK holds an estimated 19.5% market share in sports fashion, making it the clear market leader ahead of Sports Direct (Frasers Group) at approximately 14.0%.

Key market trends include the continued growth of premium running footwear (driven by Hoka and On Running), the expansion of athleisure as a daily dress category, and the increasing importance of sustainability credentials in brand choice. The market is also seeing pressure from direct-to-consumer channels operated by Nike and Adidas, though to date this has primarily affected smaller retailers rather than Tier 0 partners like JD.

The competitive landscape remains intense. Sports Direct has been investing in store fit-outs and own-brand development. Foot Locker continues to focus on its "Lace Up" strategy with a narrowed store portfolio. Online pureplays ASOS and Zalando compete in the digital channel but lack the physical retail presence that drives two thirds of JD revenue.

## 3. Channel Strategy

### Stores (68% of revenue)

The UK store estate comprises approximately 472 JD fascia stores across five tiers.

Sixteen flagship stores, including London Oxford Street, Manchester Arndale, Birmingham Bullring, and Glasgow Buchanan Street, are the primary focus for brand partnerships and exclusive product launches. These stores each target between GBP 25 million and GBP 45 million in annual revenue.

A tier stores in major city centres deliver the core volume of the business. B tier stores in shopping centres and C tier stores in retail parks serve as important community and family shopping destinations. The smallest high street stores are being gradually consolidated into larger formats where possible.

Eight new stores are planned for FY2027, all in the larger format categories. Approximately 10 to 15 underperforming smaller stores will be closed, resulting in a net reduction of 2 to 7 stores overall.

### Online (33% of revenue)

The online channel, comprising jdsports.co.uk and the JD mobile app, is planned for GBP 1.75 billion in revenue. Average order value is targeted at GBP 82.00, with a conversion rate of 5.0%.

Click and collect is expected to account for 25% of online orders, driving additional footfall into stores. The JD Status loyalty programme, with a target of 500,000 paying members by January 2027, is a key initiative to improve customer retention and repeat purchase rate.

## 4. Category Targets

### Footwear (55% of revenue)

GBP 2.92 billion target. Gross margin of 46.5%.

Footwear is the core of the JD proposition. The category benefits from strong brand loyalty, high average transaction values, and the cultural significance of sneaker drops and limited editions. The running footwear subcategory is the fastest growing segment at 14% year-on-year, driven by technical innovation from Nike, ASICS, Hoka, and On.

The premium price band (RRP over GBP 90) represents the largest share of footwear revenue and the highest margins. The entry price band under GBP 60 is served primarily through own-brand McKenzie and selected opening-price-point product from core brands.

### Apparel (38% of revenue)

GBP 2.01 billion target. Gross margin of 49.5%.

Apparel delivers higher margins than footwear due to the strength of own-brand product (McKenzie, Supply & Demand, Montirex, Pink Soda Sport) and higher initial markups on branded clothing. The athleisure and streetwear segments are the primary growth drivers. Outerwear from The North Face is a major seasonal contributor in the autumn and winter months.

### Accessories (7% of revenue)

GBP 371 million target. Gross margin of 51.0%.

Accessories, including bags, caps, socks, and shoe care, deliver the highest margins in the business. The category benefits from strong add-on purchase behaviour, particularly when promoted through mechanics such as 3 for 2.

## 5. Brand Targets

### Nike (40% of revenue, GBP 2.12 billion)

Nike remains the anchor brand of the JD portfolio. The partnership operates under a Tier 0 framework agreement that provides JD with exclusive colourways, first access to limited drops, and dedicated shop-in-shop space in flagship stores. The planned gross margin on Nike product is 46.8%, with lifestyle product (Air Force 1, Air Max, Dunk) delivering the strongest sell-through rates at 85% to 95%.

### Adidas (25% of revenue, GBP 1.33 billion)

Adidas is the second largest brand, with strength across Originals lifestyle product, running, and training. The partnership includes exclusive colourway access and early delivery on key seasonal launches. Planned gross margin is 47.5%.

### New Balance (6% of revenue, GBP 318 million)

New Balance continues to grow strongly, driven by the 990 series, the 530, and the Fresh Foam running range. The brand has seen 18% year-on-year growth in JD UK and commands higher margins than the core brand average due to its premium positioning.

### The North Face (5% of revenue, GBP 265 million)

The North Face is the dominant outerwear brand in the portfolio, with peak sales in the autumn and winter months. The Nuptse jacket is consistently one of the top 10 selling SKUs across the entire business.

### Own Brand (8% of revenue, GBP 424 million)

Own-brand product, including McKenzie, Supply & Demand, Montirex, and Pink Soda Sport, delivers gross margins of 54% to 58%, significantly above the branded average. Growing own-brand penetration from 8% to 10% of revenue is a key strategic priority, as it directly improves overall margin performance.

### Other Brands (16% of revenue, GBP 848 million)

This group includes Puma, Vans, Converse, Under Armour, ASICS, Hoka, On, Timberland, Lacoste, Tommy Hilfiger, and approximately 40 other brands carried in the JD range.

## 6. Regional Targets

The business is managed across 11 UK regions.

London and the South East together represent 30% of planned revenue at GBP 1.59 billion. This region has the highest density of flagship stores and the most significant online penetration.

The North West, JD's home region with headquarters in Bury, represents 14% of revenue at GBP 742 million. The region benefits from strong brand awareness and a high concentration of large-format stores including Manchester Arndale and the Trafford Centre.

Scotland, at 10% of revenue or GBP 530 million, has shown recent volatility with some underperformance linked to store closures and stock allocation issues. The Glasgow Buchanan Street flagship and Edinburgh Fort Kinnaird store are the key revenue drivers, each targeting over GBP 25 million.

The Midlands, Yorkshire and the North East together account for 27% of revenue. The South West, Wales, Northern Ireland, and the East of England make up the balance.

The target for regional like-for-like growth ranges from 1.5% in mature regions such as the South East to 4.0% in growth regions such as Scotland and the North East where new flagship investments are expected to drive outperformance.

---

## 7. Monthly Phasing

Revenue is heavily weighted towards the second half of the financial year, with Q4 (November to January) accounting for 31% of annual sales.

December is the single largest month at approximately GBP 770 million, driven by Christmas gifting and the end-of-season full-price trading period before the Boxing Day sales commence.

August, boosted by the Back to School campaign, is the second largest month at approximately GBP 480 million.

The quietest trading period is February and March, with combined revenue of approximately GBP 635 million, representing just 12% of the annual total.

Promotional calendar highlights include the January sales (GBP 313 million in clearance), the Summer Sale in July (GBP 425 million), Back to School in August (GBP 480 million), and Black Friday in November (GBP 560 million).

## 8. Strategic Initiatives

Five strategic initiatives underpin the FY2027 plan.

**JD Status Loyalty Programme.** Target of 500,000 paying members by January 2027. The programme offers early access to product drops, exclusive member-only product, double points on own-brand purchases, and free delivery. The expected incremental revenue contribution is GBP 35 million, with improved customer retention as the primary benefit.

**Store Refit Programme.** Twelve stores will undergo full refurbishment in FY2027, following the twelve completed in FY2026. Refitted stores have historically seen a 15% to 25% uplift in sales in the 12 months following completion.

**DC Automation.** A GBP 15 million investment in warehouse automation at the Kingwood distribution centre, expected to reduce cost to serve by 12% and improve stock accuracy to 99.5%.

**Online Experience Upgrade.** A GBP 8 million investment in the JD website and mobile app, including improved personalisation, faster checkout, and enhanced click and collect functionality.

**Own-Brand Expansion.** Increasing own-brand penetration from 8% to 10% of revenue through expanded ranges in McKenzie womenswear and a new Supply & Demand accessories line.

## 10. Risks and Mitigations

**Nike Direct-to-Consumer Expansion.** Nike continues to invest in its own retail channels. Mitigation is through the Tier 0 partnership agreement, exclusive product access, and the strength of JD's physical retail footprint which Nike cannot replicate at scale.

**UK Consumer Confidence.** Pressure on household disposable incomes may impact discretionary spend on fashion. Mitigation includes a strong own-brand value proposition and flexible promotional planning.

**Supply Chain Disruption.** Global shipping volatility and port congestion remain risks. Mitigation includes diversified sourcing, increased buffer stock on key SKUs, and the dual DC network.

**Competitor Activity.** Sports Direct and Foot Locker continue to invest. Mitigation is through brand partnerships, exclusive product, and the JD Status loyalty programme.
